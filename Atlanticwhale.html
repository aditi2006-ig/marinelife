ABOUT NORTH ATLANTIC RIGHT WHALE

The North Atlantic right whale (Eubalaena glacialis) is one of the most endangered whale species, primarily found along the eastern coast of North America. Known for their slow swimming and distinctive callosities on their heads, these whales can reach lengths of up to 55 feet and weigh up to 70 tons. Historically, they were targeted by whalers for their blubber and baleen, leading to severe population declines. Today, their greatest threats include ship strikes, entanglement in fishing gear, and habitat degradation. Conservation efforts focus on reducing human impacts, such as implementing shipping lane adjustments and promoting safer fishing practices, in hopes of increasing the population and protecting this vital species in marine ecosystems.

Where North Atlantic Right Whale Are Found

It is now mostly found along the Atlantic coast of North America, Two other species of right whales exist: the North Pacific right whale, which is found in the North Pacific Ocean, and the Southern right whale, which is found in the southern hemisphere.
