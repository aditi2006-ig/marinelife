Kelp Forests

Kelp forests are underwater ecosystems dominated by large, fast-growing seaweeds called kelp, which thrive in nutrient-rich, cold coastal waters. These forests provide critical habitats for a variety of marine species, including fish, invertebrates, and marine mammals. Kelp can grow up to 2 feet per day, forming dense underwater forests that provide food, shelter, and breeding grounds for numerous species.Kelp forests are important for coastal protection, as they help absorb wave energy, reducing erosion and protecting shorelines. They also play a role in carbon sequestration, as kelp absorbs carbon dioxide during photosynthesis. However, kelp forests are vulnerable to threats such as climate change, pollution, and overfishing, making their conservation crucial for maintaining marine biodiversity and ecosystem services.
