GALAPAGOS PENGUIN

ABOUT GALAPAGOS PENGUIN

Galapagos penguins are highly social birds that often forage in groups for fish, squid, and crustaceans, relying on their agility and speed in the water to catch prey. They are known for their unique behavior of swimming in shallow waters and diving to depths of around 30 meters (about 98 feet). Breeding typically occurs throughout the year, depending on food availability, with nests often located in volcanic crevices or under rocks to protect their eggs from predators. However, the Galapagos penguin faces threats from climate change, invasive species, and human activities, making conservation efforts essential to ensure the survival of this unique species in its fragile ecosystem.
