Mangrove Forest

Mangrove forests are coastal ecosystems found in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by salt-tolerant trees and shrubs that thrive in brackish water where freshwater meets the ocean. These unique forests are well adapted to the harsh, waterlogged conditions with specialized root systems like aerial roots and pneumatophores that help them breathe in oxygen-poor soils. Mangroves provide crucial ecological services, acting as natural barriers against coastal erosion, storm surges, and rising sea levels while fostering rich biodiversity.
