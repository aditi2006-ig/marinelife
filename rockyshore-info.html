Rocky Shore

Rocky shores are coastal areas where solid rock formations meet the ocean, creating a dynamic and challenging environment for marine life. These shores are shaped by constant waves, tides, and weathering, leading to the formation of tide pools, crevices, and cliffs. The intertidal zone, where the land meets the sea, is divided into different sections based on water exposure, influencing the types of organisms that can survive there.

Rocky shores play an essential role in coastal ecosystems by providing breeding grounds and shelter for marine organisms. They also act as natural barriers, protecting coastlines from erosion. However, human activities such as pollution, overfishing, and climate change threaten these habitats, making conservation efforts crucial to preserving their biodiversity.
