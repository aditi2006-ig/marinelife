VAQUITA

The Vaquita is a small porpoise, measuring about 4 to 5 feet long. It has a streamlined body, dark gray coloration with lighter underparts, and distinct facial markings. Its small, triangular dorsal fin and rounded flippers enhance its agility in water. The skull is adapted for echolocation, allowing it to navigate and hunt effectively in its Gulf of California habitat.

Where Vaquita Are Found

Vaquitas are only found in the shallow waters of the northern Gulf of California, Mexico. This area is abundant in fish and shrimp, and many people depend on fishing for their livelihood.
